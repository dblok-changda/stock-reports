## 1. Key News Events & Analysis (Past Week)

### 🔴 Elon Musk's $158 Billion Compensation for 2025 (Multiple Sources: WSJ, Bloomberg, Quartz)
This is by far the most widely discussed TSLA news item this week. The disclosure that Musk's 2025 compensation totaled **$158.4 billion** (per the new 10-year moonshot package approved by shareholders) generated intense social media debate.

**Key nuance:** As Quartz noted, this figure is "almost entirely an accounting-driven valuation" — **no shares have vested** and Tesla confirmed Musk's **realized pay was zero**. However, the optics are problematic:
- **Bull case:** The package is structured to incentivize Musk to hit massive milestones (including growing TSLA's market cap to $8.5 trillion), aligning his interests with shareholders.
- **Bear case:** The $158B accounting charge hits Tesla's income statement, and the headline number fuels narratives of corporate governance risk and insider enrichment. Social media reaction was heavily negative on this point.

**Sentiment Impact: NEGATIVE** — The headline number is staggering and dominates conversation, even though the realized compensation is $0. Retail investors express frustration over dilution concerns.
