# TSLA Technical Analysis Report — May 1, 2026

## Executive Summary

TSLA is in a **sustained multi-month downtrend** that has erased approximately **22%** from its December 2025 highs (~$490). While a sharp oversold bounce from the April 7 low of $337.24 has lifted the stock ~13% to $381.63, multiple technical indicators suggest this rebound is **losing momentum** and faces significant overhead resistance. The bearish moving average alignment, fading MACD histogram momentum, and price action below all major averages paint a cautionary picture.

### Key Observations:

**Bearish Stacked Alignment:** The 10 EMA ($378) < 50 SMA ($384) < 200 SMA ($402) forms a fully bearish "stacked" alignment, indicating short, medium, and long-term trends are all pointing lower. This is the classic structure of a sustained downtrend.

**50 SMA as Immediate Resistance:** Price touched the 50 SMA area on Apr 17 (intraday high of $409.28) and was decisively rejected. The 50 SMA has been declining since mid-March and currently sits at $384 — just $2.50 above the current close. This is the **critical near-term resistance** to watch.

**200 SMA as Major Overhead:** The 200 SMA at $402 represents a massive ceiling. Even if TSLA reclaims the 50 SMA, it must then contend with this longer-term benchmark. The 200 SMA has been flattening and slightly rising (from ~$360 in early January to ~$402 now), but the price has been below it since early February, creating a **prolonged bearish regime**.

**10 EMA Curling Up:** A modest positive: the 10 EMA has turned upward from $355 in mid-April to $378 now, indicating the short-term trend is improving. However, for a genuine trend change, the 10 EMA must cross above the 50 SMA — a signal that remains distant.

**Death Cross Watch:** The 50 SMA ($384) is declining and the 200 SMA ($402) is rising/flattening. If the 50 SMA continues descending and crosses below the 200 SMA, it would form a **death cross** — a powerful bearish long-term signal.

## Verdict

The weight of evidence points to a **fading bounce within a persistent downtrend**. The bearish moving average alignment, rapidly deteriorating MACD histogram, RSI stuck in neutral, and rejection at the 50 SMA all suggest that TSLA's April rebound is running out of steam. The most likely path is a resumption of the downtrend, with the MACD histogram crossing below zero as the proximate trigger. The 10 EMA at ~$378 is the last short-term support — losing it would open the door to retests of the April lows.

FINAL TRANSACTION PROPOSAL: **SELL**

Traders should consider exiting long positions or initiating shorts with a stop above the 50 SMA (~$384) and targets at $365, $352, and $337. Position sizes should be reduced given the elevated ATR environment.
