High-NA EUV machines are ASML's most expensive product at ~$400 million each and represent the next major revenue growth driver. A delay from TSMC—ASML's largest customer—directly impacts the revenue ramp timeline for this critical product line. *(Source: Insider Monkey)*

- **TSMC's A13 Process Could Sidestep High-NA Entirely:** Even more alarming, TSMC's A13 process node and N2U technology could potentially bypass the need for ASML's $400 million High-NA machines. Barchart reports this raises **fundamental questions about ASML's moat** and could disrupt the entire chip industry if proven viable at scale. If TSMC—ASML's largest single customer—can achieve leading-edge nodes without High-NA, the long-term total addressable market for ASML's premium product shrinks materially. *(Source: Barchart)*

- **Post-Earnings Dip Despite Solid Results:** Both TSMC and ASML reported solid earnings but saw shares dip, suggesting **market expectations are extremely high** and priced for perfection.
