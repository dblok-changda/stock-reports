### Theme 1: The TSMC High-NA EUV Question — Existential or Overblown?

This is the most critical issue facing ASML. TSMC's dual announcements — (1) delaying high-NA EUV deployment to 2029+ and (2) developing A13/N2U processes that could bypass high-NA entirely — directly threaten ASML's most profitable and technologically differentiated product line.

**Bull Counter-Argument:** 
- Samsung and SK Hynix are still ordering aggressively, providing demand diversification.
- Even if TSMC delays, it hasn't canceled — 2029 is still in the pipeline.
- ASML's standard EUV machines remain indispensable and have no substitute.

**Bear Argument:**
- TSMC is ASML's single largest customer and the technology leader. If TSMC doesn't need high-NA, other foundries may follow suit.
- A process that achieves similar results without high-NA machines fundamentally undermines ASML's pricing power and competitive moat.
- The delay to 2029 pushes out a major revenue stream by years, impacting growth expectations.

**Assessment:** This is a legitimate long-term risk that the market has not fully priced in. The near-term order book remains strong, but the long-term moat narrative has been cracked.
