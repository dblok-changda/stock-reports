**Recommendation**: Underweight

**Rationale**: After carefully weighing both sides of this debate, I'm landing on Underweight for ASML. The bear's arguments carry more weight in the near term, though the bull's structural thesis prevents me from going to a full Sell.

Here's how I see it: ASML's monopoly position and the AI capex supercycle are real — the bull is right that TSMC's multi-patterning workaround doesn't bypass ASML entirely, and the $19.6B backlog provides meaningful visibility. The service revenue annuity is a legitimate growth lever when utilization spikes.

But the bear wins on three critical fronts. First, the High-NA delay is not just noise — it directly undermines the ASP expansion narrative that underpins the 58% forward EPS growth consensus. If that growth rate gets revised down (and it likely will, given TSMC's strategic pivot), the 30x Forward PE re-expands and the stock faces a violent de-rating. Second, the valuation offers zero margin of safety: 47x TTM PE, 2.14 PEG, 1.5% FCF yield, and $1B in buybacks at all-time highs during a negative FCF quarter is a red flag. Third, the technical picture genuinely favors the bears — distribution volume on down days, bearish RSI divergence, 10 consecutive negative MACD histogram sessions, and an asymmetric risk/reward (roughly 8% upside to $1,543 resistance vs. 15% downside to $1,207 measured move target).

The bull keeps saying "the double-top is unconfirmed" — and that's technically true — but unconfirmed patterns don't justify adding risk. The setup is warning, not confirming, and you don't need to wait for the house to burn down to step outside. The critical point is that at 47x trailing earnings with deteriorating momentum and a challenged growth narrative, the risk/reward is skewed to the downside right now. This could change — if Q2 FCF snaps back and the technicals reverse, the bull thesis reignites. But right now, prudent positioning means reducing exposure.

**Strategic Actions**: 1. Reduce ASML position by roughly 20-30% of current holding size. This isn't an exit — the monopoly and AI demand thesis has structural merit — but trim enough to meaningfully lower portfolio risk given the unfavorable near-term setup.

2. Set a hard stop-loss at $1,365 (the double-top neckline). A break below this level confirms the bearish pattern and targets $1,207. If triggered, reduce remaining position by another 50%.

3. If the stock holds the 50 SMA ($1,399) and shows a bullish MACD crossover with expanding volume on an up-day, consider rebuilding the trimmed portion. The coiling thesis isn't dead — it just needs confirmation before committing capital.

4. Monitor two catalysts closely: (a) Q2 earnings for FCF snap-back and management commentary on High-NA demand from Intel/Samsung, and (b) any revision to the 58% forward EPS consensus. If EPS estimates start getting cut, the valuation trap the bear describes becomes real and further reduction is warranted.

5. Re-entry framework: If ASML pulls back to the $1,200-$1,300 range (where the FCF yield improves and the double-top measured move is complete), aggressively add back. The structural bull thesis becomes compelling at a better entry point — just not at current levels with current technical headwinds.
